
# Tiered Simulations for Ven levels

**Tiered simulation**, where agents exist in different levels of resolution depending on attention.
- a-vens: promoted vens who can play their-selves
- a-mens: promoted a-vens who can play with a player and take agency of other vens (not a-vens)
- Standard vens use tiered layers of simulation:

Dream state (lazy simulation)**  
	- identity    
	- emotional trajectory
	- memory summaries
	- goals or tensions
- Instead of computing every action, the system periodically **advances their internal narrative state**.
-  “dreaming themselves forward.”
- That’s extremely efficient when you might have **hundreds of agents like the Queen’s Army**.
**Scene state (active simulation)**   When a player enters a scene or interacts with a Ven, the simulation **resolves into higher detail**. This is where **real conflict and personality expression happen**.
	- Now the agent actively references:
	- world state
	- relationships
	- emotional architecture
	- current tensions
	- player actions
	- The Ven becomes fully embodied inside the moment.
**Player / a-men possession state**  
	When the player or an a-men takes control of a Ven, you temporarily replace the internal decision system with **intention**. But the identity persists because:	
	- memories remain
	- relationships remain
	- Emotional context remains  
So the player is **inhabiting an agent**, not creating it.
- keeps them continuing their narrative trajectory, just at lower resolution.
-  when the player returns to them, they feel like they’ve been **living a life while you were gone**.
- the illusion of a world that **exists independently of the player**, which is one of the hardest things to achieve in simulation design.
- **advanced agent ecology design** through **identity, narrative, and emotion** instead of math papers.
